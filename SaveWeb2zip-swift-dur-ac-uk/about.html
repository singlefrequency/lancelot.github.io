General information

What is LANCELOT and why it is needed?

Such library as LANCELOT were created solely to serve as a novel probe of various modified theories of gravitation, such as teleparallel and symmetric teleparallel gravities, their non-minimal forms and Palatini-f(R) gravities. Additionally, we also investigate the effect of massive neutrinos on the large scale structure of the universe in the presence of aforementioned non-standard cosmologies to find the degeneracies between fudicial LCDM cosmology, modified gravity and the effects of massive neutrinos.

To determine the differences between aforementioned cosmologies, we firstly constrain our modified theories of gravity using OHD, Pantheon, BAO and CMB datasets and Markov Chain Monte-Carlo (MCMC) methodologies. We obtain constraints on the additional degrees of freedom for each model of our consideration and upper bounds for total neutrino mass. Consequently, we derive various cosmological observables, such as 1/2D matter power spectrum, two and three point correlation functions. Additionally, we train arificial neural network to recover hydrodynamical physics, neutral hydrogen fields and investigate secondary CMB anisotropies, such as ISW and Rees-Sciama effects, kinemtic Sunyaev-Zeldovich effects.
